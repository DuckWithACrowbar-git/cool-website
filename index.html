Linux is an open-source operating system made by Linus Torvalds as an alternative to paid OSes (like MS-DOS and MacOS).

He wanted something people could trust, and made the Linux kernel as a result. Other people made their own versions

of Linux called "Linux distributions" or "Linux distros". The most popular Linux distros for beginners are Ubuntu and

Linux Mint because they have a lot of utilities and tools installed with the operating system. (Wikipedia page)
